To examine whether architectural modifications can reduce high-frequency parameter variability in S4D-based dPL, this study proposes **an architecture-modified S4D family**, denoted as S5D (**Smoothed Structured State Space model**). The key idea is to improve the temporal behavior of learned dynamic parameters by modifying the **encoder architecture**, while keeping the **loss function, optimizer, and training configuration unchanged**.

The S5D family mainly investigates the role of a **Conv1D-based local temporal smoothing module**. Two placement variants are considered. In **δMGS5Dv1**, the 1D convolutional layer is placed **after the S4D backbone and immediately before the parameter output layer**. This output-side design is intended to **encourage local temporal continuity in the representations used to generate dynamic hydrological parameters**. In **δMGS5Dv2**, the 1D convolutional layer is placed **before the S4D backbone and is applied to the input embedding**. This input-side design aims to smooth and enrich local meteorological features before they enter the state-space sequence encoder.

In the initial S5D design, **LayerNorm and a rescaled Softsign activation were also considered as candidate stabilizing components**. LayerNorm was tested as an alternative to BatchNorm for normalizing sequential latent representations, while Softsign was introduced to reduce boundary saturation in the normalized dynamic parameter outputs. **To isolate the individual and combined effects of these components, we conducted a complete 2×2×2 factorial ablation over convolution, normalization, and activation choices. This design allows the contribution of Conv1D, LayerNorm, and Softsign to be evaluated separately rather than inferred only from the comparison between S5D placement variants.**
